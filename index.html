Pens

short vanilla projects

3D Responsive Welcome

There's a dev shop in Salt Lake by the name of Dingg. Their homepage is a great example of some of the ways CSS can be used to make a site passively interactive. This pen is inspired by a welcome heading on their site. I took the idea and tried to not only make the text respond to the cursor, but also to add weight to the cursor location.

See the Pen 3D Welcome by John Meade (@johnmeade-webdev) on CodePen.

Spinning Flower

This is a brief dive into java-script driven CSS keyframes. An animation caught my eye as I was watching a video on youtube and I thought I'd give it a go in CSS. The idea is that a simple circle fans itself out to display related colors throughout the spectrum; each disc then moves together as it spins in place.

See the Pen Spinning Flower by John Meade (@johnmeade-webdev) on CodePen.

Neumorphic Nav

It's not 2020 if it's not Neumorphic! This style is popping up across the web, partially inspired by the the Roli Keyboard. As a musician, I love the fact that a modern instrument is gaining traction not only through its unique sound control, but also through aesthetic. Here's a simple nav-bar with in Neumorphic style.

See the Pen Neumorphic Nav by John Meade (@johnmeade-webdev) on CodePen.
